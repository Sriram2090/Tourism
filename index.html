HERITAGE ATTRACTIONS

Brihadeeswarar Temple

UNESCO World Heritage Site, this magnificent 1000-year-old temple built by Raja Raja Chola I is a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture with its towering vimana and intricate sculptures.

Location

Thanjavur Maratha Palace

The royal palace complex houses the Saraswathi Mahal Library with ancient manuscripts, the Art Gallery with exquisite paintings, the Royal Museum showcasing Chola.

Location

Thanjavur Art Gallery

Experience the world-famous Thanjavur paintings with their rich colors, compact composition, and gold foil work. The gallery showcases traditional and contemporary Tanjore art forms.

Location

FESTIVALS & CELEBRATIONS

Brihadeeswarar Temple Festival

The annual festival celebrating Lord Shiva at the Big Temple features music and dance performances, rituals, and cultural programs that attract devotees worldwide. The grandeur of the temple illuminated with lights adds divine charm to this celebration.

All Details

Classical Dance Festival

Annual Bharatanatyam and classical dance festival celebrating the rich dance heritage of Thanjavur, featuring performances by renowned artists from across India in temple courtyards. This vibrant festival attracts tourists worldwide.

All Details

Music Festival

Annual music festival honoring saint-composer Tyagaraja, featuring Carnatic music concerts, competitions, and cultural programs that celebrate Tamil Nadu's musical heritage. Musicians and devotees from around the world gather here, creating a soulful atmosphere of devotion and art.

All Details

ARTS & CULTURE

Thanjavur Dolls

Famous for the unique bobblehead dolls made from terracotta, these traditional handicrafts represent the artistic excellence of Thanjavur craftsmen and are popular souvenirs for visitors.

All Details Location

Musical Instruments

Thanjavur is renowned for crafting the finest veenas and other classical musical instruments. Visit workshops where master craftsmen create these beautiful instruments.

All Details Location

Chola Bronzes

Admire the exquisite bronze sculptures of the Chola period, including the famous Nataraja statues. These masterpieces showcase the pinnacle of South Indian bronze casting artistry.
